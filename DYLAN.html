Types of vehicles

There are two common types of vehicles in space travel. Rockets, and rovers. Rockets are used for the actual travel to the destination and rovers are used for the exploration once you reach the destination. Both are very important components for sucessful missions in space.

Past and Current Rockets:

A famous saying is, “It’s not rocket science.” This saying correctly entails that rocket science is an extremely difficult science. The rockets of today display that difficulty and what has been accomplished is tremendous. This level of ingenuity is shown in spacecrafts like the U.S.'s Space shuttle. A clever procedure is when the rocket uses a multi-stage fuel dispatching process to save weight but still use the same amount of fuel. In this procedure, there are three distinct phases of a rocket launch. First is the powered ascent. Here, the 3 thrusters are all active as it propels upwards. The second phase is the discarding of the two extra fuel tanks while the main continues thrust. The third and final stage is the ejecting of the lower portion of the main rocket. Then, the rocket continues on to the destination.
