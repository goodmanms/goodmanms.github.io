Types of vehicles

There are two common types of vehicles in space travel. Rockets, and rovers. Rockets are used for the actual travel to the destination and rovers are used for the exploration once you reach the destination. Both are very important components for sucessful missions in space.

Past and Current Rockets:

A famous saying is, “It’s not rocket science.” This saying correctly entails that rocket science is an extremely difficult science. The difference of rockets of today and past rockets display that difficulty and what has been accomplished is tremendous. The first rocket launched in July of 1950. It was used for testing int he upper athmosphere. .Skip forward in time some years and there is the U.S.'s Space shuttle. A clever procedure is when the rocket uses a multi-stage fuel dispatching process to save weight but still use the same amount of fuel. In this procedure, there are three distinct phases of a rocket launch. First is the powered ascent. Here, the 3 thrusters are all active as it propels upwards. The second phase is the discarding of the two extra fuel tanks while the main continues thrust. The third and final stage is the ejecting of the lower portion of the main rocket. Then, the rocket continues on to the destination. Programs like NASA use clever proceses like these to make these missions successful. However, not all space travel is with the government. On Sunday, February 19 at 9:39:00 a.m. EST. SpaceX launched the Falcon 9 rocket carrying the Dragon cargo spacecraft. The Falcon 9 rocket was designed to return astronauts to space and thus it was designed for reliability. An astonishing event that took place after the launch was when the 1st stage returned and landed upright on a platform.
