Hydroelectric Energy

What is Hydroelectric Energy


Hydroelectricity is the term referring to electricity generated by hydropower; the production of electrical power through the use of the gravitational force of falling or flowing water. Hydroelectricity is the most used renewable energy source in the world.


What is Hydroelectric


Hydroelectric energy is renewable, which means that its is essentially unlimited. However, there’s only a limited number of suitable reservoirs where hydroelectric power plants can be built and even less places where such projects are profitable. Generating electricity with hydro energy is not polluting itself. The only pollution occurs during the construction of these massive power plants. Hydroelectricity is very reliable energy. There are very little fluctuations in terms of the electric power that is being by the plants, unless a different output is desired. Countries that have large resources of hydropower use hydroelectricity as a base load energy source. As long as there is water in the magazines electricity can be generated.
