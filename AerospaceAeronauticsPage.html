Careers in Aerospace Aeronautics

Our Destination is Mars. But to get there, you cant do it alone. Masters in Softward, Hardware, Structures, and Propulsion Engineering, as well as professionals in Launch and Test will be required to make the journey to mars. While there may only be a few astronauts, the team is takes to get those astronauts to the planet will take many hard working and dedicated individuals, each with a specific role that will greatly contribute to colonization of Mars. This page is here to inform you of the most necessary jobs required to Reach our final destination, the Red Planet.

Software Engineering

Some of the highest starting salaries in the country and unparalleled hiring appetites from employers, it should come as no surprise that software engineering is one of the most in demand professional fields in the country. Some of the jobs that must be performed are creating code that autonomously pilots our vehicles in space, simulates countless flight scenarios, translates petabytes of data, performs computational fluid dynamics, and more.

Hardware Engineering

Computer hardware engineers research, design, develop, and test computer systems and components such as processors, circuit boards, memory devices, networks, and routers. These engineers discover new directions in computer hardware, which generate rapid advances in computer technology. Nearly every board and box that controls, communicates, actuates, or powers our avionics is built in-house employing the same iterative approach used in the commercial industries, will be created by a Hardware Engineer.
