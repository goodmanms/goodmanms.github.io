The first 3D printing technologies were introduced in the late 1980s. At that time, the 3D printing was called Rapid Prototyping (RP) technologies. This type of technology was originally used as a fast way to create prototypes for product development in industry.  Dr. Kodama in Japan filed the first patent for RP technology in May of 1980. However, the full patent specification was not filed before the one-year deadline after the application, which was disastrous because Dr. Kodama was a patent lawyer! In real terms, 3D printing can be traced back to 1986, when Charles (Chuck) Hull issued a patent for stereolithograhy apparatus (SLA), which he invented in 1983. He later went on and co-founded one of the largest organizations in the 3D printing industry today – 3D Systems Corporation.

         3D Systems’ first commercial system was the SLA-1, introduced in 1987 and following testing, the first of these systems were sold in 1988.
