Wind Energy

What is Wind Energy?

Wind is a type of solar energy. Wind is created by the uneven heating of atmosphere by sun, the special way our earth’s surface is and the rotation of the earth. Wind power is generated by converting moving energy from the wind to mechanical power.

What are the Pros and Cons of Wind Energy?

Pros of Wind Energy

First,  wind energy is a renewable resource so we can use  it for as long as we want. It is also a source of clean, non-polluting electricity.  Second, Wind is cost effective. Wind is one of the cheapest renewable energy sources today costing between four and six cents per kilowatt-hour. Third, wind turbines can be built in rural areas. Farmers & ranchers can still have their space for crops since the wind turbine will only take a fraction of the area. Also, the owners of the wind turbine pay rent to the farmer or rancher for using the land providing them with additional income. Finally, the energy from wind creates jobs. In recent years, the wind sector invested more than 8 billion dollars of private capital in the United States economy. More than 73,000 workers were employed because of wind energy.

 

Cons of Wind Energy

To begin, wind power may not be as cheap as we believe. Depending on how energetic a wind site is, it may not be cost competitive. Even though the cost of wind energy has decreased dramatically over ten years, the technology requires more money at the start than fossil-fueled generators. Next, radar interference by wind turbines though rare & usually avoided is still a risk. The United States and the British military have found ways to avoid this challenge, so it’s not as crucial. Finally, wind power plants have received concerns about the noise produced by turbine blades, visual impacts to landscapes & wildlife such as birds and bats being killed.
